How much does penetration testing cost in Rwanda?

It is one of the first questions every organisation asks, and one of the hardest to find a straight answer to. Most security firms in Rwanda and across East Africa do not publish pricing, which makes it difficult for IT managers and procurement teams to budget for security testing.

This guide gives you transparent ranges based on real market pricing, explains what drives costs up or down, and helps you understand what you should expect at each price point.

What affects the price?

Several factors push costs up or down:

Scope and complexity

A simple brochure website with a contact form costs far less to test than a core banking application with hundreds of API endpoints, role-based access control, and transaction processing. The number of user roles, authentication mechanisms, and integrations all increase testing time.

Tester qualifications

An OSCP-certified tester with banking experience commands higher rates than a junior analyst running automated tools. You are paying for expertise, not just time. The difference in findings quality between a senior manual tester and a scanner is substantial.

Testing methodology

Black box testing (no credentials provided) takes longer than grey box (standard user credentials provided) or white box (full access including source code). Most engagements use grey box as it provides the best balance of realism and coverage.

Reporting requirements

Some organisations need a basic technical report. Others need executive summaries, compliance mapping (BNR, PCI DSS, ISO 27001), remediation workshops, and retesting. These additional deliverables add to the cost.

On-site vs remote

Internal network testing requires the tester to be physically present. If your provider is based outside Rwanda, travel and accommodation costs get added. Working with a Kigali-based provider eliminates this overhead.

International vs local pricing

International firms (Europe, US, South Africa) testing Rwandan organisations typically charge USD 15,000 to 50,000+ for comprehensive engagements. This includes travel, higher hourly rates, and international overhead.

Local or regionally-based providers with equivalent qualifications can deliver the same quality at 40-60% lower cost because they eliminate travel expenses, operate with lower overhead, and understand the local technology landscape (USSD, mobile money, local banking platforms).

The key is ensuring the local provider holds recognised certifications. OSCP is the benchmark. If a provider is OSCP-certified and has experience with your industry, the quality of work should be equivalent regardless of where they are based.

How to budget for penetration testing

If you are an IT manager or CISO building a security budget for your Rwandan organisation, here is a practical approach:

  • Minimum viable programme: USD 5,000 to 8,000 per year for annual web application and external network testing. This meets basic BNR requirements.
  • Recommended programme: USD 12,000 to 20,000 per year for quarterly web/API testing plus annual comprehensive assessment including internal network and mobile apps.
  • Enterprise programme: USD 25,000+ per year for continuous testing, managed security, and ongoing vulnerability management. Suitable for large banks and organisations with complex environments.

Compare this to the cost of a data breach. IBM's 2025 Cost of a Data Breach report puts the average at USD 4.88 million. Even a fraction of that impact would dwarf the cost of regular testing.

Red flags in pricing

Watch out for these warning signs when evaluating proposals:

  • Extremely low prices (under USD 1,000 for a "full pentest"): you are getting a scanner report, not manual testing
  • Pricing per vulnerability: this creates perverse incentives to inflate findings or miss them entirely
  • No mention of methodology: legitimate providers reference OWASP, PTES, or OSSTMM
  • No sample report available: if they cannot show you what the deliverable looks like, be cautious
  • No certifications listed: if the tester has no OSCP, PNPT, or equivalent hands-on certification, question the depth of testing

Getting a quote

To get an accurate quote, prepare the following information for your provider:

  • What systems need testing (web apps, APIs, mobile apps, network, USSD)
  • Number of applications and approximate number of pages/endpoints
  • Number of user roles per application
  • Whether credentials will be provided (grey box vs black box)
  • Any compliance requirements (BNR, PCI DSS, ISO 27001)
  • Preferred testing window and any blackout periods
  • Whether retesting is required after remediation

A qualified provider will review this information and come back with a detailed proposal including scope, methodology, timeline, and fixed pricing within 48 hours.
